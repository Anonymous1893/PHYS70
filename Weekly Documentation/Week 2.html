The Story So Far... (with the box)

The 3D model

I messed around with the 3D model for ages because I wanted to make sure I got all the parameters right. The time was definitely well spent because I now had a box net where I could change everything parametrically. This made it really easy to scale down a smaller version of my box and also very easily update that "working prototype".

The number of fingers

Kerf adjustments

Attempt 1

It cut well and the sides interlock well, but the fingers did not hold because of the kerf. Doing this first cut helped me gain some confidence with the laser cutter and get comfortable with the steps involved.

Attempt 3

I tried the 0.2mm offset again but it was a little bit too tight for the teensie teeth to handle. The scoring also happened at an odd location which made it difficult to fold the box correctly.

Attempt 2

I tried a model with a 0.2mm offset but I may have gotten a bit too confident because I didn't put the correct laser cutter settings in. Scoring lines didn't really score and it ended up being a throwaway cut

Attempt 4

I dialed the kerf offset to 0.12mm but that let to a gap that was slightly too big and the box was still opening a little. To fix the issue with the folding, I cut this box at a 45 degree angle to the "grain" of the cardboard and that solved the issue basically perfectly! Having the kerf offset be parametric was a life saver because it meant that I could make a bunch of adjustments

Printing the final box

I dialed the kerf offset up a little to 0.15mm but it ended up being worse at sticking than the smaller one was (even though the offset was bigger?)!
Nothing a bit of tape can't fix though.

I think I didn't account for bigger boxes having more "give" due to the bigger fingers, maybe in the next version I'll give 0.2mm another shot.
