Process

Creating this Final Project was definitely a process. One that I am really proud of.

If you look at some of the early weeks from this website, you'll see that most of my focus was on the Sisyphus component. i.e. making a robot that can climb stairs. I don't think I had a good idea for difficult that would be until halfway through the semester, as you can see in my MVP reflection.

This is the direction the walking bots were going:

Focussing on the stairs, the movement, and the Pomodoro half of the project was the best decision I made for it. I ended up spending my "skill points" really honing in on the enclosure and the programming and not spending so much time on the complexities of creating a walking robot (especially at the scale of the escalator I made).

The first part was getting the LCD to work! Working with I2C was fun and I got to play


I then got set on working on the escalator mechanism. I was able to find a 3D model for the escalator here and used CAD around it to make the housing for the screen and buttons. Here are the gears and some photos of the CAD for the housing I added around it! There was a decent amount of iteration I needed to do to get the button widths and press fit sizes exactly right, but it was worth it for how great the final assembly looked

I ended up spending a good amount of this project on making this assembly robust and it's something I'm really happy with. While he may not be pushing a boulder up the hill, I can say that Sisyphus is definitely happy.
