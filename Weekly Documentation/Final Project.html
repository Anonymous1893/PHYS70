What I'm currently using:

  • THE BIGGEST REASON IT WORKS: I like the way it feels to press the button

I've downloaded every Pomodoro app under the app store (that's free), bought hourglasses, and clearly I care enough about this to buy a physical Pomodoro timer but I've always had at least one complaint about the usability.

Some "apps" I have used before: Forest, Flow, Lifeline, cuckoo.team

Concerns I've had in the past:
Sometimes it's the Software:
  • Limited flexibility with the time intervals. Focus/Break/Long-break etc.
    • Only the rich are allowed to pick how long their focus sessions are
  • Often I skip breaks when I'm in the flow but there's no system in the apps I've used to cash in those breaks later
    • I almost want a mix of Pomodoro and FLOWMADORO
  • They look "lame" or are clunky to interact with
Sometimes it's the Hardware:
  • There's definitely something more satisfying about interacting with a physical timer but with physical devices comes cost
  • They're all so minimalist and cute, not very in line with what I think of "work"
    • Work is HARD it's a constant uphill battle (opening my laptop) and a test of stamina (not switching to YouTube).
  • Even with physical timers, it can be hard to find ones that have a compelling physical representation of progress
What's wrong with what I have
  • Lacks ability to switch between break and focus modes
  • Doesn't have the mythical Pomodoro x Flowmadoro mode
  • Not enough tomfoolery

The Original Goals

Things I definitely want to have:

  • A display that reads out the timer and other useful information
  • Sisyphus going up some contraption
  • A satisfying start and stop button
  • Seamless flow from "Pomodoro" to "Flowmadoro"

Things that would be really nice to have:

  • Phone/Mac connectivity to change timer intervals and modes
  • Some sort of thing where the ball rolls back down at the break time
