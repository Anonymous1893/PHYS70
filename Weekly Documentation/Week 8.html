Moulding and Casting

For this week I tried my hand at modeling BT21's favorite lil guy:

I had initially hoped to make a 3D silicone version of his head so I made this model! It was really interesting working with so many curved lines and soft shapes in Fusion, the fillet and spline tools were my best friends through this process.

I ended up deciding to make a little desk tray modeled around his gorgeous face. I turned it 2D and put into the CNC milling machine! The heart was the main component so I used most of the 13mm depth for that (8mm deep) and an extra 2mm for the eyes and mouth. Here's how it turned out!

It looks exactly as cute as I hoped and even though it's made with pocket cuts from the DXF I made, the edges are curved because of the bit. That worked perfectly for me though.

Since the tray I wanted is also a negative, I tried my hand at vacuum forming and creating a plaster mold too! The vacuum formed mold ended up being a lot shallower than I would have wanted, probably because the plastic had trouble bending around the thing pocket cuts of the eyes. I would have preferred to mold a positive and then filled it in from underneath and not using the negative to begin with. This likely wasn't in line with how the vacuum former is intended to be used.

Nevertheless I gave the plastering a shot and it still came out really cute! It's nice how subtle the curves are, it's grown on me. I initially had the ratios for water and plaster wrong but Kassia was nice enough to help me with that. I added a bit of red paint to the mixture for the pink tone as well! I accidentally overfilled the mold so there's a bit of an edge around it which I sanded off after this photo was taken.

I think this is still a success!
